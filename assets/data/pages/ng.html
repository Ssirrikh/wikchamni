ŋawa·ʔat   v. arriving. hawtaw mukʼe·ši ʔanikʼ ŋawa·ʔat mukʼešakŋiṭʰ pahathay ʔanikʼ puno·xutʰa tʰašti When women arrive at their womanhood their parents put them on a diet. underlying form: #ŋa·wa -at. Morph: durative present.

ŋawit   v. will arrive. ʔamaʔ tʰihinʼ tʰaŋ maxta·tʰa wakʼ hinwit ʔatitaw ŋawit And they sent him to get whatever he could when he got to the bottom. underlying form: #ŋa·wa -t. Morph: future.

ŋawši   v. arrived. ʔe··· ŋawši maʔ Hey, you have arrived. ʔamaʔ tʰihikʼ ŋawši mo·yʼukʼiw And they arrived at Whirlwind place. underlying form: #ŋa·wa -ši. Morph: aorist.

ŋa·waw   v. arrival, rows of basket weaving. ʔamaʔ naʔ ŋa·waw ʔopʰoʔtow kʼənwat nahni yitʼan yow we·ʔi puŋyʼit And I sometimes put down one or two rows a day. underlying form: #ŋa·wa -0 -w. Morph: neutral verbal noun locative case.

ŋe·ʔawʼ   n. evening star.
